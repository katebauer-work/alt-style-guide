Buy-first priority

  1. Perfect black bottoms: wide-leg jeans, relaxed denim, or a leather-look midi skirt.
  2. Layering tops: black mesh, fitted turtlenecks, clean tanks, and band tees.
  3. One strong outer layer: moto jacket, long black coat, blazer, or cream trench.
  4. Impact shoes: platforms, Mary Janes, pointed flats, burgundy boots, Vans.
  5. Hardware: chain belt, wallet chain, layered silver necklaces, small structured bag.
Theme 01

Band Tee Uniform

The daily anchor

Graphic tee, black denim, metal hardware, and a shoe with attitude. This is the base layer that makes the rest of the wardrobe feel like you.

Prioritise buying

  • Black or charcoal high-rise straight/wide jeans
  • 2–3 oversized band tees in black, grey, or white
  • Chain belt or wallet chain
  • Chunky loafers or classic Vans
  • One small black crossbody bag

How to style it

Tuck or half-tuck the tee, keep the denim relaxed, then add one hard detail: chain, studded belt, platform shoe, or layered necklace.

Theme 02

Plaid, Print & Punk Colour

Red, lime, leopard, tartan

The outfits that keep the black base but let something loud cut through: tartan trousers, leopard skirts, neon bags, red shoes, graphic prints.

Prioritise buying

  • Red or black tartan trousers/skirt
  • Leopard or acid-green printed skirt
  • Red shoe, red bag, or lime mini bag
  • Black denim or moto jacket
  • Graphic tee that can ground the colour

How to style it

Only one or two loud pieces at a time. Let the black tee, boot, belt, or jacket act as the frame.

Theme 03

Romantic Goth Skirts & Lace

Soft volume, dark texture

Long skirts, lace hems, sheer sleeves, black florals, chains, and boots. It reads dark but still soft and wearable.

Prioritise buying

  • Black lace or mesh long-sleeve top
  • Tiered black midi or maxi skirt
  • Dark floral or leopard midi skirt
  • Mary Janes or platform boots
  • Layered silver necklaces and chain belt

How to style it

Balance volume with a tucked, fitted, or sheer top. Boots keep the skirt from feeling too sweet.

Theme 04

Minimal Dark Polish

Sleek, grown, still sharp

The dressier side: black tailoring, leather-look skirts, pointed shoes, sheer layers, and clean jewellery. This is the going-out or work-adjacent lane.

Prioritise buying

  • Black blazer with real structure
  • Leather-look midi skirt or trouser
  • Pointed flats or heels in black, burgundy, or red
  • Fitted black turtleneck or mesh underlayer
  • Small structured black or red bag

How to style it

Keep the palette tight and let texture do the work: matte blazer, sheer sleeve, leather-look skirt, pointed shoe.

Theme 05

Soft Contrast Neutrals

Cream, grey, denim, metallic

A breather from all-black without losing the edge. Cream coats, grey knits, white trousers, silver skirts, denim, and red accents make the wardrobe feel less one-note.

Prioritise buying

  • Cream trench or long coat
  • Grey oversized turtleneck
  • White or cream wide-leg trouser
  • Metallic pleated skirt
  • Red bag or pointed red shoe

How to style it

Use black as the grounding layer, then add one pale or metallic piece. It keeps the outfit fresh without turning preppy.

Theme 06

Dark Academia Utility

Plaid, vests, cargo, long coats

Structured but still alt: argyle, plaid skirts, waistcoats, long coats, cargo trousers, and chunky boots.

Prioritise buying

  • Grey plaid skirt or trouser
  • Black waistcoat or sweater vest
  • Long black coat
  • Charcoal cargo or wide-leg trouser
  • Chunky lace-up boots

How to style it

Think tailored shape plus heavy shoe. Add a chain belt when it starts to feel too classic.
